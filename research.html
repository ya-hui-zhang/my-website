Research

Our group works on theoretical condensed matter physics with an emphasis on strongly correlated quantum systems. We develop analytical and numerical approaches to understand exotic quantum phases of matter and quantum phase transitions. Below are our four main research directions.

Moiré Systems

Moiré superlattices formed by stacking two-dimensional materials with a small twist angle or lattice mismatch have emerged as a remarkably tunable platform for studying strongly correlated physics. The resulting flat bands enhance interaction effects and give rise to a rich variety of quantum phases.

Our work in this area includes:

  • Fractional quantum anomalous Hall effects — We predicted fractional quantum anomalous Hall states in moiré systems at zero magnetic field, including in rhombohedral multilayer graphene and twisted MoTe2. We also proposed new classes of quantum anomalous Hall crystals in multilayer graphene.
  • Fractional quantum spin Hall effects — We introduced the concept of a vortex spin liquid that realizes a fractional quantum spin Hall effect in moiré Chern bands, and studied its non-Abelian and Abelian descendants.
  • Correlated insulators and magnetism — We have studied the nature of correlated Chern insulators, ferromagnetism, and topological electronic crystals in twisted graphene systems, in close collaboration with experimental groups.
  • Topological flat bands — We predicted nearly flat Chern bands in moiré superlattices and developed lattice models for twisted bilayer graphene aligned with hexagonal boron nitride.

Cuprate & Nickelate Superconductors

High-temperature superconductivity remains one of the central open problems in condensed matter physics. We develop new theoretical frameworks to understand the cuprate and the recently discovered nickelate superconductors.

Our work in this area includes:

  • Ancilla qubit theory of the pseudogap metal — We developed a new approach using ancilla (auxiliary) qubits to describe the transition from the pseudogap metal to the Fermi liquid in cuprates. This framework provides variational wavefunctions for the Mott insulator and the pseudogap metal that capture their essential topological properties.
  • Bilayer nickelate La3Ni2O7 — Following the discovery of high-Tc superconductivity in pressurized La3Ni2O7, we proposed a Type-II t-J model with shared superexchange coupling from Hund's rule, and studied strong pairing from a small Fermi surface beyond weak coupling. We also studied the symmetric pseudogap metal phase using a double Kondo lattice model.
  • Pair-density waves and d-wave superconductivity — We studied pair-density-wave superconductors from doping Haldane chains, and developed models of d-wave superconductivity with coexisting antiferromagnetism and charge order on the square lattice.

Fractional Phases

Quantum spin liquids are exotic phases of matter in which spins remain disordered even at zero temperature due to strong quantum fluctuations. They host fractionalized excitations and emergent gauge fields, and are closely connected to topological order.

Our work in this area includes:

  • Chiral and Dirac spin liquids — We studied chiral spin liquids, Dirac spin liquids, and their connections to topological superconductors via dualities. We also investigated SU(4) chiral spin liquids in moiré bilayers and spin liquids on the square-lattice Heisenberg antiferromagnet.
  • Quantum Hall multilayer systems — We studied the interplay of quantum Hall physics with moiré systems and bilayer structures, including the XY* transition and extraordinary boundary criticality from fractional exciton condensation in quantum Hall bilayers, exciton supersolids, and quantum Hall spin liquid states in trilayer moiré superlattices.

Numerical Methods

In addition to analytical approaches, our group actively develops and applies state-of-the-art numerical methods to study strongly correlated quantum systems. These computational tools allow us to go beyond mean-field descriptions and obtain unbiased or highly accurate results for challenging many-body problems.

Our work in this area includes:

  • Density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) — We use DMRG to study ground-state properties of one-dimensional and quasi-one-dimensional strongly correlated systems, including Kondo lattice models with ultra-local criticality and doped Haldane chains hosting pair-density-wave superconductivity.
  • Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) — We develop variational wavefunctions based on ancilla qubits and parton constructions for Mott insulators, pseudogap metals, and spin liquid states, and optimize them using Monte Carlo sampling.
  • Neural network quantum states — We explore the use of neural network ansatze as variational wavefunctions for strongly correlated systems, leveraging their expressive power to capture complex entanglement structures in quantum many-body problems.
