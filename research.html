Research

Our group works on theoretical condensed matter physics with an emphasis on strongly correlated quantum systems. We develop analytical and numerical approaches to understand exotic quantum phases of matter and quantum phase transitions. Below are our four main research directions.

Moiré Systems

Moiré superlattices formed by stacking two-dimensional materials with a small twist angle or lattice mismatch host nearly flat Chern bands where interaction effects dominate, giving rise to a rich variety of correlated and topological quantum phases.

  • Integer and fractional quantum anomalous Hall phases — We predicted nearly flat Chern bands in moiré superlattices [2] and fractional quantum anomalous Hall states in rhombohedral multilayer graphene in the moiréless limit [39]. We proposed new classes of quantum anomalous Hall crystals in multilayer graphene [47], studied Chern insulators at integer and fractional filling in moiré pentalayer graphene [42], and introduced the vortex spin liquid with fractional quantum spin Hall effect in moiré Chern bands [35] with its non-Abelian and Abelian descendants in twisted MoTe2 [38]. We also studied phase transitions out of quantum Hall states in moiré materials [32] and the continuous transition from Fermi liquid to a fractional Chern insulator [p4].
  • Mott physics in strongly correlated topological bands — We studied topological Mott localization and pseudogap metal in twisted bilayer graphene [48], moiré-driven topological electronic crystals in twisted graphene [43], mixed valence Mott insulator and composite excitation in twisted bilayer graphene [p2], and exciton- and light-induced ferromagnetism from doping a moiré Mott insulator [33]. We also studied SU(4) chiral spin liquid, exciton supersolid, and electric detection in moiré bilayers [18] and ferromagnetism in narrow bands of moiré superlattices [11].
  • Theory of superconductivity for twisted bilayer graphene — We proposed a resonating-valence-bond superconductor from small Fermi surface in twisted bilayer graphene [p9], and studied holon metal, charge-density-wave and chiral superconductor from doping fractional Chern insulator and SU(3)1 chiral spin liquid [p3]. We also studied chiral and nodal superconductors in the t-J model with valley contrasting flux on triangular moiré lattice [31] and doping a moiré Mott insulator as a model study of twisted cuprates [23].

Cuprate & Nickelate Superconductors

High-temperature superconductivity remains one of the central open problems in condensed matter physics. We develop new theoretical frameworks to understand the cuprate and the recently discovered nickelate superconductors.

  • Ancilla qubit theory of the pseudogap metal — We developed a variational wavefunction for Mott insulators at finite U using ancilla qubits [49], studied topological Mott localization and pseudogap metal in twisted bilayer graphene [48], and described the transition from the pseudogap metal to the Fermi liquid using ancilla qubits [12].
  • Bilayer nickelate La3Ni2O7 — We proposed a Type-II t-J model with shared superexchange coupling from Hund’s rule in superconducting La3Ni2O7 [30], studied strong pairing from a small Fermi surface beyond weak coupling [37], and described the strong pairing and symmetric pseudogap metal in a double Kondo lattice model [44]. We also studied the Type-II t-J model in the charge transfer regime in bilayer and trilayer nickelates [41] and doping a spin-one Mott insulator with application to bilayer nickelate [p7].
  • Non-Fermi liquid criticality with Fermi surface volume jump — We studied deconfined criticality and ghost Fermi surfaces at the onset of antiferromagnetism in a metal [13], the deconfined Fermi liquid to Fermi liquid transition and superconducting instability [36], the small to large Fermi surface transition in a single-band model using randomly coupled ancillas [16], and electronic spectra with paramagnon fractionalization in the single-band Hubbard model [20].

Fractional Phases

Quantum spin liquids host fractionalized excitations and emergent gauge fields. We study these exotic phases and their connections to quantum Hall physics, topological order, and unconventional superconductivity.

  • Quantum spin liquids — We studied Dirac and chiral spin liquids on the spin-1/2 square-lattice Heisenberg antiferromagnet [46], SU(4) chiral spin liquid, exciton supersolid, and electric detection in moiré bilayers [18], quantum Hall spin liquids and their possible realization in moiré systems [8], and spin liquids and pseudogap metals in the SU(4) Hubbard model in a moiré superlattice [6]. We also explored doping the chiral spin liquid into a topological superconductor or chiral metal [15] and charge-4e anyon superconductor from doping SU(4)1 chiral spin liquid [p6].
  • Quantum Hall multilayers — We studied the XY* transition and extraordinary boundary criticality from fractional exciton condensation in quantum Hall bilayers [28], anyon superfluid in trilayer quantum Hall systems [p5], doping a Mott insulator with excitons in a moiré bilayer [25], and bridging Hubbard model physics and quantum Hall physics in trilayer moiré superlattices [3].
  • Deconfined quantum phase transitions — We investigated deconfined criticalities and dualities between chiral spin liquid, topological superconductor and charge density wave Chern insulator [26], and studied phase transitions out of quantum Hall states in moiré materials [32].

Numerical Methods

In addition to analytical approaches, our group actively develops and applies state-of-the-art numerical methods to study strongly correlated quantum systems, going beyond mean-field descriptions to obtain unbiased results for challenging many-body problems.

  • Density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) — We study numerical signatures of ultra-local criticality in one-dimensional Kondo lattice models [34], and pair-density-wave superconductors from doping Haldane chains [24].
  • Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) — We develop variational wavefunctions for Mott insulators at finite U using ancilla qubits [49], study the small to large Fermi surface transition using randomly coupled ancillas [16], and investigate the fractional Fermi liquid in generalized t-J models [14].
  • Neural network quantum states — We explore neural network ansatze as variational wavefunctions for strongly correlated systems, leveraging their expressive power to capture complex entanglement structures in quantum many-body problems.
