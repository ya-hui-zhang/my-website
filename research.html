Research

Our group works on theoretical condensed matter physics with an emphasis on strongly correlated quantum systems. We develop analytical and numerical approaches to understand exotic quantum phases of matter and quantum phase transitions. Below are our four main research directions.

Moiré Systems

Moiré superlattices formed by stacking two-dimensional materials with a small twist angle or lattice mismatch host nearly flat Chern bands where interaction effects dominate, giving rise to a rich variety of correlated and topological quantum phases.

  • Fractional quantum anomalous Hall effects — We predicted fractional quantum anomalous Hall states in rhombohedral multilayer graphene in the moiréless limit [39], and proposed new classes of quantum anomalous Hall crystals in multilayer graphene [47]. We also studied phase transitions out of quantum Hall states in moiré materials [32].
  • Fractional quantum spin Hall effects — We introduced the vortex spin liquid with fractional quantum spin Hall effect in moiré Chern bands [35], and studied its non-Abelian and Abelian descendants in twisted MoTe2 [38].
  • Correlated insulators and magnetism — We studied moiré-driven topological electronic crystals in twisted graphene [43], Chern insulators at integer and fractional filling in moiré pentalayer graphene [42], topological flat bands in multilayer graphene moiré lattices [40], and ferromagnetism in narrow bands of moiré superlattices [11].
  • Topological flat bands and excitonic phases — We predicted nearly flat Chern bands in moiré superlattices [2], developed lattice models for twisted bilayer graphene aligned with hexagonal boron nitride [5], and studied exciton- and light-induced ferromagnetism from doping a moiré Mott insulator [33]. We also studied SU(4) chiral spin liquid and exciton supersolid in moiré bilayers [18].

Cuprate & Nickelate Superconductors

High-temperature superconductivity remains one of the central open problems in condensed matter physics. We develop new theoretical frameworks to understand the cuprate and the recently discovered nickelate superconductors.

  • Ancilla qubit theory of the pseudogap metal — We developed a variational wavefunction for Mott insulators at finite U using ancilla qubits [49], studied topological Mott localization and pseudogap metal in twisted bilayer graphene [48], and described the transition from the pseudogap metal to the Fermi liquid using ancilla qubits [12].
  • Bilayer nickelate La3Ni2O7 — We proposed a Type-II t-J model with shared superexchange coupling from Hund’s rule in superconducting La3Ni2O7 [30], studied strong pairing from a small Fermi surface beyond weak coupling [37], and described the strong pairing and symmetric pseudogap metal in a double Kondo lattice model [44]. We also studied the Type-II t-J model in the charge transfer regime in bilayer and trilayer nickelates [41].
  • Pair-density waves and d-wave superconductivity — We studied pair-density-wave superconductors from doping Haldane chains and rung-singlet ladders [24], developed a model of d-wave superconductivity, antiferromagnetism, and charge order on the square lattice [29], and studied deconfined Fermi liquid to Fermi liquid transition and superconducting instability [36].

Representative publications: [49], [48], [44], [41], [37], [36], [30], [29], [24], [20], [13], [12], [10], [1] — see full list.

Fractional Phases

Quantum spin liquids host fractionalized excitations and emergent gauge fields. We study these exotic phases and their connections to quantum Hall physics, topological order, and unconventional superconductivity.

  • Chiral and Dirac spin liquids — We studied Dirac and chiral spin liquids on the spin-1/2 square-lattice Heisenberg antiferromagnet [46], investigated deconfined criticalities and dualities between chiral spin liquid, topological superconductor and charge density wave Chern insulator [26], and explored doping the chiral spin liquid into a topological superconductor or chiral metal [15].
  • Quantum Hall multilayer systems — We studied the XY* transition and extraordinary boundary criticality from fractional exciton condensation in quantum Hall bilayers [28], quantum Hall spin liquids and their possible realization in moiré systems [8], doping a Mott insulator with excitons in a moiré bilayer [25], and bridging Hubbard model physics and quantum Hall physics in trilayer moiré superlattices [3].

Representative publications: [46], [38], [35], [32], [28], [26], [25], [18], [15], [8], [6], [3] — see full list.

Numerical Methods

In addition to analytical approaches, our group actively develops and applies state-of-the-art numerical methods to study strongly correlated quantum systems, going beyond mean-field descriptions to obtain unbiased results for challenging many-body problems.

  • Density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) — We study numerical signatures of ultra-local criticality in one-dimensional Kondo lattice models [34], and pair-density-wave superconductors from doping Haldane chains [24].
  • Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) — We develop variational wavefunctions for Mott insulators at finite U using ancilla qubits [49], study the small to large Fermi surface transition using randomly coupled ancillas [16], and investigate the fractional Fermi liquid in generalized t-J models [14].
  • Neural network quantum states — We explore neural network ansatze as variational wavefunctions for strongly correlated systems, leveraging their expressive power to capture complex entanglement structures in quantum many-body problems.

Representative publications: [49], [34], [24], [18], [16], [14] — see full list.
